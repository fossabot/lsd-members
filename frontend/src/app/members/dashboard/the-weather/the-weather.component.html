What about the weather?

Skydiving is a heavily weather dependent sport and requires good visibility, wind within an acceptable range and no rain. It also relies on having a clear view from the plane to the ground, which can cause problems with the typically cloudy British sky.

It's often the case that the forecast may look slightly hit-or-miss for a weekend but the only way you can guarantee you won't be jumping is by not turning up. You can often learn about lots of other important parts of skydiving (like packing parachutes) when the weather isn't great, and every jump counts: especially when you're just starting off.
